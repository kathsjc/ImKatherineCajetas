About

There's a moment in every conversation when someone stops performing and starts being. You can hear it. The slight shift in their voice, the way they pause before the next sentence, how the carefully constructed facade gives way to something raw and real. That moment is why TrueVoice exists.

I've spent two decades building products that connect people to experiences, stories, and each other. From the early days of mobile platforms to the current age of generative media, I've watched technology promise to bring us closer together while often leaving us feeling more isolated than ever. We have infinite ways to share our thoughts, yet we seem to have forgotten how to truly listen to one another.

TrueVoice began with a simple question: What if we created space for the stories that don't fit neatly into social media posts or elevator pitches? What if we honored the messy, beautiful complexity of human experience?

The inspiration came from an unexpected place. While interviewing candidates for various positions, I found myself more fascinated by their detours than their achievements. Not the resume bullet points, but the stories that revealed who people had become.

We live in a world obsessed with outcomes but remain starved for the narratives that create them. We celebrate the arrival but ignore the journey. We highlight the achievement but overlook the transformation.

TrueVoice is my experiment to course-correct. This isn't about famous people or extraordinary accomplishments, though those stories may find their way here too. This is about the nurse who found her calling during the pandemic, the small business owner who rebuilt after losing everything, the teenager who discovered poetry while caring for his grandmother with dementia. These are the voices that deserve to be heard, preserved, and celebrated.

Great stories have the power to transform both reader and subject. They can take an ordinary moment and reveal an extraordinary truth. They can help us recognize ourselves in strangers and find strangers within ourselves.

TrueVoice isn't trying to solve the world's problems. We're simply committed to the radical act of paying attention. To creating a space where stories can breathe and the journey is welcomed.

In a culture that often reduces people to data points and demographics, we insist on seeing individuals as they truly are: layered, evolving, worthy of our time and attention. We believe that in sharing our stories — really sharing them, with honesty and care — we create connection that transcends differences.

This is my invitation to you: to slow down, to listen, to discover the remarkable stories that surround us every day. Because everyone carries a story worth preserving, and everyone deserves to have their voice heard.

Welcome.

LaSean Smith
Founder, TrueVoice
